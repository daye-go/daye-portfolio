Redefining Enterprise Knowledge Discovery

Samsung's internal intranet operated across 300,000+ employees spanning dozens of divisions. Information existed in abundance — but insight was trapped. Employees spent hours navigating siloed systems, outdated wikis, and disconnected document repositories just to find a single policy or benchmark.

The real problem wasn't a lack of data. It was a lack of a system that could understand, connect, and surface that data in the context of actual work being done.

We were asked to design a platform that could turn Samsung's sprawling internal knowledge base into a living, responsive intelligence layer.

Project Lead
My Role

Leading UX end-to-end, from AI system architecture to pixel-perfect delivery.

As the Lead UX Designer on the MOSAIC project, I owned the full design scope — from early discovery and AI interaction modeling through to the final design system and handoff. This wasn't just an interface project; it required deep collaboration with AI/ML engineers, IBM consultants, and Samsung's internal IT architecture team.

My responsibility spanned three interconnected challenges:

1. Designing the AI interaction model — how users would converse with, guide, and trust an AI assistant embedded in their daily work.

2. Building the information architecture — structuring how RAG-powered retrieval would be surfaced across departments, roles, and document types.

3. Crafting the full MOSAIC platform UX — a unified intranet experience combining knowledge discovery, ideation support, activity feeds, and AI summarization.

Research
Designing an AI Assistant That Turns Internal Knowledge into Insight

Information was abundant. Insight was scarce.

In large organizations, knowledge doesn't disappear — it fragments. Field research with Samsung employees revealed a consistent pattern: the right information existed somewhere, but finding it required navigating across 6–12 different internal tools, asking colleagues in Slack, or simply giving up.

What employees told us:

Users didn't describe technical problems. They described frustration moments — specific situations where the gap between "I need to know X" and "I can find X" cost them hours. The most painful: cross-department knowledge transfer, onboarding to new projects, and preparing materials for executive reviews.

Two outcomes redefined the project direction:

Retrieval alone wasn't enough: Users needed the AI to not just find documents — but to understand context, connect relevant pieces across silos, and proactively surface what they didn't know to search for.

Trust required transparency: Employees were skeptical of AI outputs. Any system that couldn't show its reasoning — where did this come from, how confident is it — would be ignored.

Design
What We Built: MOSAIC RAG + LLM Platform

The insight that shaped everything: employees don't search for documents — they search for answers.

We built an AI Assistant powered by RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) and LLM that not only retrieves data from internal systems but understands the company's context — policies, workflows, and project structures.

"Unlike conventional chatbots, this assistant acts as an active collaborator: it finds relevant materials, summarizes key points, and connects insights across departments."

The MOSAIC platform delivered three distinct AI-powered experiences:

M-Pilot (AI Copilot): The core AI assistant, embedded across the platform. Users could ask natural language questions, request document summaries, generate ideation prompts, and receive proactively curated recommendations — all grounded in verified internal sources.

Insight Radar: A personalized knowledge feed that surfaced relevant internal documents, project updates, and cross-department learnings based on each user's role, ongoing projects, and interaction patterns.

MOSAIC Ideation: A structured space for collaborative brainstorming, where the AI could join sessions, suggest related precedents from the knowledge base, and help teams move from raw ideas to structured proposals faster.

I designed the end-to-end flow — from how users initiate AI interactions, to how confidence scores and source citations are displayed, to how the system escalates to human experts when it reaches the boundaries of its knowledge.

After launch, MOSAIC became the most-used internal tool across pilot divisions within 3 months.

Users specifically cited the AI's ability to connect information they didn't know was related — surfacing precedents from adjacent teams that saved weeks of duplicated research.

Lasting Impact: From Information Retrieval to Organizational Intelligence

The framework that made MOSAIC successful was deceptively simple:

Find → Connect → Surface → Act

Not: search for a document.
But: understand what a user is trying to accomplish — and bring the organization's collective knowledge to bear on that goal.

This project demonstrated how RAG + LLM integration can transform enterprise knowledge systems from static repositories into a living intelligence layer — where information doesn't just answer questions, it sparks ideas and accelerates decisions across 300,000 people.

Beyond knowledge retrieval. Genuinely useful intelligence.
