The Problem Nobody Was Asking Out Loud

Toss had built one of the most usable fintech apps in Korea. Competitors were catching up on usability. And as they did, Toss's once-distinctive edge was quietly disappearing.

The harder question wasn't how users interacted with the product — it was why they still hit friction when it actually mattered. We had optimized for ease of use, but not for ease of life.

We had been asking the wrong questions.

My Role

As the DRI on the Moment Silo, I owned the design end-to-end — from research strategy through shipping — while leading cross-squad collaboration with product, engineering, and the research team.

The Moment Silo operates differently from other squads at Toss. Where each squad owns a specific feature — individual trees — Moment owns the connective tissue across all of them. My responsibility was to design for the gaps between features, not within them. That meant the biggest risk wasn't building something unusable. It was building something that solved the wrong problem entirely.

Research
Redefining How We Listen

The first thing we changed was our research model.

Traditional usability testing tells you where users struggle with your product. It can't tell you where your product isn't even in the room when users need help most.

Working with the research team, I co-led the design of a direct VOC (Voice of Customer) channel — a lightweight, in-app mechanism to ask users about real friction moments in their financial lives, not just their experience with Toss features. The design challenge was reducing the barrier to honest response: not a survey, but a conversation.

What we got back shifted the project's direction.

Users didn't describe usability problems. They described moments — specific situations where their financial life got harder, and where no product had ever showed up for them.

Two outcomes emerged beyond the data itself:

Authentic voices: We heard genuine pain points that quantitative metrics had consistently masked.

Co-creation: Users told us they felt like they were building Toss with us. That sense of participation builds trust in a way satisfaction scores don't capture.

Design
What We Built: Cardless ATM Withdrawal

The insight that led to this feature wasn't subtle once we saw it.

Users didn't mentally separate "having a card" from "accessing their money." When the card was missing, the money felt unreachable — even with a phone in hand. The user's mental model was: card = access. And no one had redesigned that assumption.

"I needed cash urgently but forgot my card. The ATM was right there, useless."

This came up repeatedly in VOC — disproportionately in high-stress, time-pressured moments. Urgency made the friction worse.

The solution was cash withdrawal with just a phone. No physical card required.

I designed the end-to-end flow: in-app withdrawal initiation, OTP-based ATM authentication, and the surrounding UX handling edge cases — low balance states, authentication failures, nearby ATM lookup. The constraint was real: adding a step to a cash-access moment can't feel like adding a step.

After launch, it landed as one of the most positively received features in the Moment Silo's history.

Users specifically cited it when asked what made Toss feel different from other fintech apps. It wasn't praised for being clever. It was praised for solving something that had been annoying people for years.

For context: Toss at the time had reached 19.1M MAU, with users opening the app an average of 270 times per month. A feature that reduces genuine financial anxiety compounds in value with every one of those sessions.

Lasting Impact: A Framework That Stayed

This process became the operating model for every product decision the Moment Silo made afterward.

Listen → Validate → Solve

Not: what can we build efficiently?
But: where does a user's life get harder — and can Toss be there?

The distinction matters. Features built from business opportunity or technical capability get used. Features built from real user struggle get relied on. The cardless ATM wasn't a feature users discovered in a settings menu. It was something they reached for the moment they needed it.

Beyond easy to use. Genuinely useful.

Also in Moment Silo
Financial Content Feed

Making Finance Easy: Creating the World's Most Accessible Financial Articles

Alongside the cash access work, Moment Silo addressed a different kind of friction: the intimidation users feel around financial knowledge itself. Many users described finance as a domain that wasn't built for them — too complex, too jargon-heavy, too easy to get wrong.

We designed an article feed that makes financial education approachable, and connects educational content directly to Toss's core services at the moment a user is ready to act — bridging understanding and action in a single flow.

The goal wasn't just content. It was reducing the distance between knowing and doing.
